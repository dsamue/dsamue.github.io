Simon



This is a group project within the course Physical Interaction Design at KTH and was developed during autumn 2017. Simon is a mimicking (almost dancing) robot that wants to be your cheerful playmate. He waves his ear for you when you make one of four gestures in front of him. So if you move your hand to the left, his ears will also move to the left. Take a look at the video to get a sense of Simon's personality!

This is the first project we made with the Arduino platform and also the first project that included a physical artifact. In this projekt I've mostly worked with the concept, interaction design and physical prototyping.

Group members: David Samuelsson, Emma Klint, Frida Eklund, Josefine Möller

Enviromind


This is a group project within the course Information Visualization at KTH and developed during spring 2017. Enviromind lets you compare CO2 emissions and trade balance around the world and since 1960. The idea is to raise awareness that CO2 emissions is a global problem and that we can not only look at our own emissions when we trade with the rest of the world.



What happens with CO2 emissions if we look at emissions per capita or in total for each land? And how does different countries compare to each other over time? What is happening in China in the 21th century?

For this project I've worked with the web development and has for example done the interaction between the timeline and responding visualizations, the functional parts of the CO2 bar chart and worked with color coding of the map.

This project will compete at the C Awards in may 2017 and is nominated for the category "Best Research Potential". You can see the current project demo online here

Group members: David Samuelsson, Frida Eklund, Viktor Gustavsson, Emma Klint, Victor Gustavsson, Josefine Möller, Fredrik Spansk, Gustav Höglund

AR-Business Card


In a more digital world than ever, the classic business card is starting to get old. The limited physical space of the card leaves little room for information and thanks to smartphones, we no longer need to keep our contacts on physical cards.

This project aims to investigate how augmented reality can be used to extend the information printed on a business card by developing a prototype and evaluate it. When a business card is viewed through the camera of a smartphone additional information will show and link you to social media, show a portfolio or give a short audio presentation of your new business contact.

The prototype is developed in Unity with the Vuforia SDK and a small user evaluation has been conducted to test how the application would work in a typical scenario. The evaluation shows that the users are positive to the product but that further development can be done to create a more intuitive experience.

This project was an introduction to working with AR in the course Multimodal interaction and interfaces and the development was a tight group process in every step of the way.

Language Web App


Fabulous Swedish is a web application to learn Swedish. Listen to a sentence and try to recreate it by listening to the words and moving them to the bubble in the right order. Follow your progress and collect points as you go. Avoid making mistakes though..

This is a project in the course Interaction Programming using the MVC concept and developing strictly with AngularJS.

The project was done in the course Interaction Programming and was basically my first attempt to make an dynamic website using Angular.js. This was a tight group project and everyone worked collectively with the development.

Take a look at the code at GitHub.

Group members: David Samuelsson, Sanna Marklund, Max Dyrhage, Robin Setterwall Gabrielsson

Museum Application Prototype


This was a project in the introduction to Human-Computer Interaction at KTH where we were given the task to make a visit to a museum a more engaging experience. After making field studies at Fotografiska we choose to develop a prototype which gives the user access to additional information about the piece of art and the possibility to save it as a favorite .

By using iBeacons for positioning the user is given information about the art that they're standing nearby. We used POP (now a part of Marvel) to make the initial prototype and then continued with proto.io for a more HIFI prototype.

During the process we worked with field studies, think-alouds, personas, scenarios and user evaluation.

Group members: David Samuelsson, Pernilla Romanus, Arvid Sätterkvist, Rasmus Bergrén, Matilda Schaffer
